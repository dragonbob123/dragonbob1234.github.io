Animals in Islam

Animals in Islamic Stories

One of the most famous stories in Islam that involves animals, in this case the spider. The story takes place when prophet muhammed was trying to run away to madina and he and his companion Abu Bakr hid in a cave late at night. One of the best trackers in Arabia was following him. The tracker and his team followed the Prophet and Abu Bakr to the cave where they hid. Meanwhile a spider had made a web in front of the cave entrance and doves nested at the front of the cave. This gave the cave an abandoned look and the tracker and his group moved on. Another story of how Animals helped Prophets was in the story of Prophet Ibraheem and the ram. Prophet Ibrahim was commanded by Allah to sacrifice his own son, when he went to do this however, he found himself slaughtering a ram. Allah had send the ram in place of ibrahim so his son didn’t have to die.(6)

Another famous example of animals in islamic stories is the story of surah al feel. In this story an evil king who wanted to destroy the kaaba. He had an army of elephants and he set out towards makkah. But, when the elephants got close, they refused to attack the kaaba. Then Allah sent a very large amount of birds to stone the whole army. As it says at 105/5 “Leaving them like chewed up leaves”. One last story is the story of Prophet Yunus. Prophet Yunus was trying to leave his people and went on a boat. The boat capsized in a storm and a whale swallowed the Prophet. Prophet Yunus then spent his time in the whale asking for forgiveness from Allah for leaving his people. Eventually the whale spit him out on the beach. He then thanked Allah for releasing him and went back to his people.(6)

Pets in Islam

Animals are allowed in islam as pets and beasts of burden. Many Sahaba used to keep animals like dogs for hunting and protection. They would keep cats for companionship. But there are rules in islam against having dogs. This is because they are made out Shaytan’s spit. Therefore they are not allowed inside the house. We are allowed however to keep birds and trim their feathers, Abu Hurayra used to do this. As muslims we can keep and tame animals like horses and camels to do hard labor for us.(5)

Animals Rights in Islam

Islam strongly asks Muslims to treat animals with compassion and not to abuse them. The Holy Qur’an states that all creation praises God in their own language. Prophet Muhammad often chastised his Companions who mistreated animals, and spoke to them about the need for mercy and kindness. It is haram to kill animals for sport or to kill an animal without there being a good reason for it (such as self defence). In the quran, Allah says that it is haram for humans to hunt animals unless they need the food. Eid al Adha is a celebration where muslims sacrifice animals. It is not necessary to sacrifice animals but it is a sunnah.(2)

What Animals are Halal

As Muslims we have to eat halal. The meat also has to be Zabiha. For something to be Zabiha it has to be be slaughtered in a special way while reciting a special dua, this does not apply to fish or plants as well as crustaceans . There are many animals that are haram regardless of how they are slaughtered.

List of Haram things

  1. Animals that have been clearly and explicitly prohibited in the Qur’an or Sunnah are without doubt Haram, such as a swine, donkey, etc.(4)
  2. Animals that are born and live in water are all Haram with the exception of fish. Also if the animals has died naturally at sea it is haram to eat the body.(4)
  3. The third principle is that, amongst the land-animals, those that have no blood in them are considered Haram, such as a hornet, fly, spider, beetle, scorpion, ant, etc.(4)
  4. The forth principle is that those animals that do not have flowing blood, are also considered Haram, such as a snake, lizard, chameleon, etc. are haram(4)
  5. The fifth principle is that all types of pests (hasharat al-Ardh) are also considered Haram, such as a mouse, hedgehog, jerboa, etc.(4)
  6. The sixth principle is an animal that eats meat or the flesh of other animals. This includes any preditory animal or omniovre(4)

Definition Of Words

Halal
denoting or relating to meat prepared as prescribed by Muslim law(1)
Haram
forbidden or proscribed by Islamic law.(1)
Animal Rights
rights believed to belong to animals to live free from use in medical research, hunting, and other services to humans.(1)
Prophet
a person regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the will of God.(1)
Islamic Law
a religious law forming part of the Islamic tradition.(1)
